Notes for Week 1, Day 1

Try to stick as closely as possible to the recommended sets/reps/times/rests. For the movements that are higher intensity (jumps, squats, etc.), feel free to rest a little longer if needed. Everything should be slow and controlled, unless otherwise specified. Ideally, aim for the concentric (the hard part) to be a little faster, and control on the eccentric (the easy part).

For the weighted movements, try to use a weight that is difficult but not super hard. For example, if you need to do 8 reps, aim for a weight that you could JUST do 12 reps of. HOWEVER, take the first few sessions light and focus on technique, timing, and getting a good burn.

For any exercises marked optional, only skip if you don't have time/if they hurt. Even if the intensity is low, it is good to expose the body to new movements.

For the first segment in blue, try to do the exercises barefoot. It is good for the feet/ankle muscles to stop relying on shoes for support.

Rolling Arm Bar w/ Screwdriver Rotation
Rolling Arm Bar w/ Screwdriver Rotation
2 sets, 5 reps
 2 5 --:-- Superset

This drill works miracles for building hip internal rotation mobility, but the details can be a bit tricky. Its ok if you dont get it down day 1.

Follow the directions in the video but I want you to especially think about the following tips when you perform the exercise.

The leg that is down on the ground must be actively pushed into the floor throughout the entire exercise. Dont lose pressure in that leg or you wont get the hip mobility benefits.

Breath in through the nose as you roll over, and out through the mouth when you roll back to the starting position.

Keep the knee that is down fully locked out and the foot dorsiflexed (toes pulled up towards your head).

Make sure the additional weight is light, between 10 and 20 lbs.

Scalable Impact Durability
Scalable Impact Durability
3 sets, 8 reps, 00:45 rest
 3 8 00:45

Use 4-6 inch box, step or bumper plate.

Level 1= lift front foot, push into the box just hard enough to step up onto the box (not a jump yet).

Level 2= start in same position, now strike the box hard. Still not jumping.

Level 3 = now start with leg high (thigh should be parallel to the floor in starting position). Strike the box hard. Still not a jump.

Level 4 = start in same position as level 3, strike the box hard and jump 20-40% of maximal jump height.

Level 5 = start in same position as level 3 and 4, strike the box hard and jump as high as possible (100% max jump height)
