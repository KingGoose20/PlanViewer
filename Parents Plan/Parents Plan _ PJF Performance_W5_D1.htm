Notes for Week 5, Day 2

-Today's first workout is focused on upper body and core and the second workout is focused on light full body mobility to drive blood flow and synovial fluid.
-Ideally these two workouts will be separated by at least 6 hours. However, if you don't have the time to separate them, you can perform both workouts at the same time. Use workout 2 as a warmup for workout 1 if you cannot separate them. The reason we separate them is because it's good for your body to accumulate movement throughout the day instead of all at once.
-For the isometrics in workout #2, I want you to get through at least 2 sets, but if you have no pain and you're not overly sore from previous lower body days then you can do up to 4 sets.

-Lay on your back and bend your knees slightly.

-Drive your heels into the ground to lift your hips off the floor until you achieve full hip extension. Hold in this position for the recommended set time.

-You should feel the burn in your hamstrings, not your lower back. If you feel it in your lower back you are likely hyperextending the spine instead of extending the hips.

Rolling Arm Bar w/ Screwdriver Rotation
2 sets, 5 reps
 2 5 --:-- 2 rds

This drill works miracles for building hip internal rotation mobility, but the details can be a bit tricky. It’s ok if you don’t get it down day 1.

Follow the directions in the video but I want you to especially think about the following tips when you perform the exercise.

The leg that is down on the ground must be actively pushed into the floor throughout the entire exercise. Don’t lose pressure in that leg or you won’t get the hip mobility benefits.

Breath in through the nose as you roll over, and out through the mouth when you roll back to the starting position.

Keep the knee that is down fully locked out and the foot dorsiflexed (toes pulled up towards your head).

Make sure the additional weight is light, between 10 and 20 lbs.

Notes for Week 5, Day 6
