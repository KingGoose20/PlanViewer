Knee Pumps
Knee Pumps
1 sets, --:--:--
 1 --:--:-- --:-- alternate

Level 1 = bodyweight, very small knee bend.

Level 2 = bodyweight, deeper knee bend (usually around 60 degree knee bend as shown in the demonstration of the video.

Level 3 = hold dumbbell in the goblet position (beneath chin, in front of chest). Each session use a weight that challenges the quads without causing knee pain. If we can reach an 8 or 9 out of 10 burn without spiking knee pain, we are working at a good level.

If you don't have the band or something to anchor it to, you can do a wall sit (shown at the 3:10 mark in this video).

If level 1 causes pain, try to hold onto chairs or bands for assistance to de-load your bodyweight (make yourself lighter) and use a very small knee bend.

If that still causes pain, then skip this exercise and use a knee pump instead.
2 Leg Hamstring Iso
2 Leg Hamstring Iso
2 sets, 00:00:30, 00:30 rest
 2 00:00:30 00:30

-Lay on your back and bend your knees slightly.

-Drive your heels into the ground to lift your hips off the floor until you achieve full hip extension. Hold in this position for the recommended set time.

-You should feel the burn in your hamstrings, not your lower back. If you feel it in your lower back you are likely hyperextending the spine instead of extending the hips.

-If you cannot achieve an 8/10 burn in the hamstrings with just your bodyweight, you can move to level 2 by adding weight on top of the hips.

-Find a weight that allows you to achieve an 8/10 burn with no pain in the knees and back

Alternate Sport

Part of becoming a great/durable athlete is expanding your movement vocabulary and strengthening different movement patterns. Today I want you to put down the basketball and go play another sport. Play a sport that you enjoy and feel free to switch up the sport week to week or month to month. We usually encourage our athletes to play tennis, spike ball, soccer or anything else that they enjoy.
