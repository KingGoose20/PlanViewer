Notes for Week 4, Day 1

Time to get some BIG GAINS! Let's get to work!! A few notes for today’s workout before you get started:
-I don't want you to get overly sore from this workout. We want to feel a LITTLE soreness tomorrow, but if we're overly sore then we did too much too soon.
-As always, skip any exercise (or choose the regression) if it causes pain or discomfort.
-If you're advanced and have been highly active for the last month, it's ok to add sets wherever you feel you need for some extra work.
-Remember, you have to train according to your current level. This is a general blueprint, but YOU have to know when to skip certain things that may not fit your current level. Training hard is important, but training SMART is key!

The journey begins now! Let's GO!

Notes for Week 4, Day 1

-Today's first workout is focused on upper body and core and the second workout is focused on light full body mobility to drive blood flow and synovial fluid.

Use workout 2 as a warmup for workout 1 if you cannot separate them. The reason we separate them is because it's good for your body to accumulate movement throughout the day instead of all at once.

Rolling Arm Bar w/ Screwdriver Rotation
2 sets, 5 reps
 2 5 --:-- 2 rds

This drill works miracles for building hip internal rotation mobility, but the details can be a bit tricky. It’s ok if you don’t get it down day 1.

Follow the directions in the video but I want you to especially think about the following tips when you perform the exercise.

The leg that is down on the ground must be actively pushed into the floor throughout the entire exercise. Don’t lose pressure in that leg or you won’t get the hip mobility benefits.

Breath in through the nose as you roll over, and out through the mouth when you roll back to the starting position.

Keep the knee that is down fully locked out and the foot dorsiflexed (toes pulled up towards your head).

Make sure the additional weight is light, between 10 and 20 lbs.

Notes for Week 4, Day 1

-Today's first workout is focused on upper body and core and the second workout is focused on light full body mobility to drive blood flow and synovial fluid.
-Ideally these two workouts will be separated by at least 6 hours. However, if you don't have the time to separate them, you can perform both workouts at the same time. Use workout 2 as a warmup for workout 1 if you cannot separate them. The reason we separate them is because it's good for your body to accumulate movement throughout the day instead of all at once.
-For the isometrics in workout #2, I want you to get through at least 2 sets, but if you have no pain and you're not overly sore from previous lower body days then you can do up to 4 sets.

Scalable Impact Durability
3 sets, 8 reps, 00:45 rest
 3 8 00:45

Use 4-6 inch box, step or bumper plate.

Level 1= lift front foot, push into the box just hard enough to step up onto the box (not a jump yet).

Level 2= start in same position, now strike the box hard. Still not jumping.

Level 3 = now start with leg high (thigh should be parallel to the floor in starting position). Strike the box hard. Still not a jump.

Level 4 = start in same position as level 3, strike the box hard and jump 20-40% of maximal jump height.

Level 5 = start in same position as level 3 and 4, strike the box hard and jump as high as possible (100% max jump height)
